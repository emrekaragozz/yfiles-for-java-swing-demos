Step 16: Snapping

The snapping feature simplifies the interactive arrangement of graph elements. It helps to align nodes, ports and bends and to distribute them evenly.

In addition to this, labels with certain label models can snap to particular positions relative to their owner, for example to the corners of a node or along the edge path. This can be enabled through class LabelSnapContext.

To see the effects of the snapping feature

  • Drag a node near another node to make it snap to the corner or center of the other node.
  • Drag a node between two other nodes to position it at the halfway point between them.
  • Resize a node to the exact size of another node.
  • Drag a bend near a node or another bend to see it snap. This makes it very easy to create orthogonal edge segments by hand.
  • Drag a node label to the border of its owner node and an edge label along the path of its owner edge.

See the sources for details.
